what inspired mist?
there’s sites like Craigslist where you can post about your “missed connection.”there’s apps like Reddit where you can post anonymously about anything.

but there was no app to reach out anonymously with the intent of connecting in real life.…until mist :)
when am i anonymous?
when you post a mist, you’re 100% anonymous. at all other times, you’re not anonymous. more specifically:
‍
- when you post a mist, you’re 100% anonymous. share your thoughts without anyone knowing it’s you.
‍
- when you reply to a mist, you’re not anonymous. the author can see your profile and choose whether to accept your dm.
‍
- when you comment on a mist, you’re not anonymous. your friends can tag you if they find one about you.
someone replied to my mist! now how can I trust who they are?
we verify that each mister is a usc student by checking their usc email upon signing up.

we ensure everyone has only one account by associating it with their phone number.

soon, we’ll be offering profile verification by confirming your profile pic with a selfie.

when you start chatting with someone, you can be sure that they really are who they say they are.
how are mists organized?
on the feed, you can always find more mists if you scroll further down.

on the map, you can only see the top 100 “trending”, “new”, or “best” mists in a given area.

you can always view your favorites, mentions and submissions under your account.
what are mist’s goals?
at mist, our mission is to be a place where real connections can form through anonymous posts. each feature we add and each decision we make is in this direction.

we all appreciate when someone writes kind words about us or takes an interest in us. we hope mist becomes a place you can spread this feeling by posting about those you know or saw in real life. a place where you can make your friend’s day, take a chance on your missed connection, and help you friends connect with others.
